## Government Databases to Monitor

### Primary (Most Inventory)

1. **GSA Auctions** (Federal)
   - URL: gsaauctions.gov
   - Inventory: Massive (seized, surplus, excess)
   - Categories: All (sanitizers, tech, furniture)

2. **GovDeals.com** (State/Local/Federal)
   - URL: govdeals.com
   - Inventory: Regional auctions
   - Categories: All (very active for sanitizers post-2020)
   - Payment: PayPal, credit card

3. **IQBid** (Government surplus specialist)
   - URL: iqbid.com
   - Inventory: State, county, city auctions
   - Categories: Electronics, office, industrial
   - Payment: Credit card

4. **Liquidity Services** (Government contractor)
   - URL: govliquidation.com
   - Inventory: Federal vehicles, equipment, office supplies
   - Categories: All
   - Payment: Credit card

5. **FEMA Disaster Supplies**
   - URL: fema.gov/disaster (check for auctions)
   - Inventory: Post-disaster liquidation
   - Categories: Sanitizers, supplies, equipment
   - Payment: Varies

## Important Notes

### Shipping
- Government auctions: Buyer pays shipping
- Some items: Free local pickup (cheaper!)
- Consider shipping cost in margin calculation

### Condition
- "As-is" â†’ Research condition carefully
- "Good" = Cosmetic wear, fully functional
- "Excellent" = Like new
- "Fair" = Works but visible damage (may not sell on Amazon)

### Amazon FBA Restrictions
- Some items banned (aerosols, hazardous)
- Sanitizer sprays: Check restrictions (may be FBA-restricted)
- Liquids: Extra fees
- Electronics: Condition-dependent

### Profitability Reality
- **Sanitizers:** Better margins if buying in massive bulk
- **Tech:** Best margins (often 40-60%)
- **Furniture:** Heavy (shipping kills margin)
- **Tools:** Good margins (25-45%)
